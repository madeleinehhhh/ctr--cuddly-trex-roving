### A Reading List

Formation happens through many means — prayer, community, daily practice, and the slow work of reading books that change how you think.

This is a living list of books that have shaped CTR's theology and practice, offered to anyone who wants to go deeper into the foundations of reconciliation, ecumenism, and intentional Christian community. It is not exhaustive. It is a place to start.

The list is organized by theme. Most books will be useful to readers from any tradition. Where a book comes from a particular tradition and assumes that perspective, we have noted it.

*The [[ctr-practices-book-of-the-month|Book of the Month]] is drawn from this list and updated regularly — a way to read along with the community if you want a more guided path.*

Books that have shaped CTR's understanding of prayer — particularly contemplative, liturgical, and intercessory prayer.

Books that address Christian formation, spiritual disciplines, and the shape of a life oriented toward God.
