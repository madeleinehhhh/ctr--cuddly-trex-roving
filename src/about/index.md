### About Christ the Reconciler

We are a missional community of prayer and reconciliation in Elgin, Texas — sixteen acres of land, a shared household, and a growing network of Christians who believe that the divisions fracturing the Body of Christ are not inevitable.

We are not trying to dissolve our differences. We are trying to end the hostility.

%%SECTION: WHO WE ARE — plain orientation%%
### Who We Are

Christ the Reconciler is a residential intentional community founded by Thomas and Amy Cogdell, a Protestant-Catholic married couple whose own life together has been a living experiment in the reconciliation they preach.

Our community includes Protestants, Catholics, Orthodox Christians, and Messianic Jews. We don't agree on everything — and we're not trying to. What we share is a common commitment to the prayer of Jesus in John 17: that his people would be one. Not uniform. One.

We gather daily for prayer. We share meals and work. We welcome guests for retreats. We produce teachings and resources for Christians everywhere who are tired of the hostility and ready to do something about it.

### Are We Orthodox? Are We Safe?

We get it. An ecumenical intentional community can raise eyebrows. So let's be direct.

Christ the Reconciler is rooted in historic, orthodox Christianity. Every member of our community is an active, committed member of their own church, parish, or congregation — Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, or Messianic Jewish. We do not ask anyone to leave their tradition. We do not create a new denomination. We are not a replacement for your local church.

We affirm the ancient faith shared across the Christian world. Our community members hold real theological differences — and we believe those differences matter and deserve honest engagement, not papering over. What we renounce is not difference, but hostility: the contempt, the dismissiveness, the assumption that a Christian from another tradition is an enemy or a fool.

We have been commended by Catholic priests and Protestant pastors, by Pentecostals and Anabaptists, by leaders in the US and across Europe. [[ctr-about-friends|Read their words →]]

### What We Believe

We don't hold a lowest-common-denominator faith. We hold *our* faiths — Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, Messianic — and we hold them seriously, with conviction and love.

What brings us together is not agreement on every doctrine. It is a shared conviction that Jesus meant what he prayed, and that Christian hostility is a sin worth repenting of. We believe reconciliation is not a feeling or a metaphor — it is a concrete set of actions that removes hostility, repairs damage, and restores relationship.

The ancient faith of the church — the Trinitarian God, the incarnation, death and resurrection of Jesus, the work of the Holy Spirit — is the ground we stand on together. Beyond that, we hold our differences with honesty and mutual respect, not pretending they don't exist, and not letting them become weapons.

### How People Relate to CTR

One of the most important things to understand about Christ the Reconciler: there is no pressure to go deeper than God is calling you. There are many ways to be part of what we're doing.

**Friends, supporters, and distant participants** — People who follow along, pray with us, listen to our teachings, and support the work financially. This is wonderful and essential.

**Statement of Personal Devotion** — Each year, individuals have the opportunity to make a personal commitment to living out John 17 in their own life. This is a meaningful but accessible step, renewed annually, and it doesn't require moving to Elgin. [[ctr-practices-john17|Learn more →]]

**Community Commitment** — A deeper level of commitment to living out John 17 together as a community, currently being developed.

**Residential Life** — When housing is complete, there will be a Residential Rule for those who are called to live here full-time. This is one expression of commitment — not the goal for everyone.

No one is pushed toward the next level. God does that.
