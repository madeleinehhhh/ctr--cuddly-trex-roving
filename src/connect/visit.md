%%SECTION: HERO / OPENING%%
### Come and See

The best way to understand Christ the Reconciler is to visit.

No description of the community — however careful — does what a shared meal or a morning prayer session does. Come and see what it means to live and pray across genuine Christian difference. Come and experience the land, the people, and the common life.

---

%%SECTION: WHAT A VISIT LOOKS LIKE%%
### What a Visit Looks Like

When you visit CTR, you are welcomed into the common life — not treated as a spectator of it. You pray when the community prays. You eat when the community eats. You are a guest, but you are a guest in a home, not a client at a conference center.

Visits typically include:

- Morning and evening prayer with the community
- Shared meals
- Conversation with community members
- Time to walk the land, rest, and reflect

Retreats may include more structured elements — teaching sessions, guided reflection, small group conversation — depending on the retreat's focus and who is visiting.

%%SECTION: WHO VISITS%%
### Who Comes

CTR welcomes individuals and groups — curious newcomers, distant supporters, Christian leaders, church groups, campus ministry teams, and anyone who wants to experience ecumenical community firsthand.

You do not have to share CTR's theology to visit. You do not have to be considering joining the community. You are welcome to come and see.
